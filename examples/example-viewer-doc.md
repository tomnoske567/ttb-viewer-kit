## Your Business Context

- **What you sell:** 12-week online strength program for men over 35, $79/month
- **Price point:** Low-ticket subscription
- **Stage:** ~9K Instagram followers, 34 paying members, wants to stop relying on referrals

---

## Who They Are

**A dad in his late 30s or 40s with a demanding job, who used to be fit, hates what he sees in the mirror, and has failed at "getting back into it" at least three times.**

### Demographics
- **Gender split:** ~90% male (from member records, n=34)
- **Age range:** 35-52, clustered at 38-45
- **Geography:** US and UK mostly (member records)
- **Income situation:** Comfortable — money is not the objection, time is
- **Primary platform they find you on:** Instagram (reels)
- **What they can afford:** $79/month is a non-decision; they've spent more on gear they never used
- **Personality patterns:** Ex-athletes, competitive at work, embarrassed to be beginners again

### Their Backgrounds (Ranked by How Often You See Them)
1. Corporate managers / office jobs (~15 of 34 members)
2. Tradesmen who went into management and stopped moving (~8)
3. Ex-college athletes (~6)

---

## The Archetypes

### 1. The Ghost of Fitness Past
Was genuinely fit at 25. Still identifies as "a fitness guy" but hasn't trained consistently in a decade. The gap between self-image and mirror is the pain.

> "I look at photos from my wedding and I don't recognize myself. And it's not even the weight, it's that I used to be the fit one in my group." — Mark, first call

### 2. The Time-Poor Provider
Wants to train but genuinely believes he has no time. Every previous program failed because it assumed 6 sessions a week.

> "By the time the kids are down it's 8:30 and I've got nothing left. Every plan I've bought was written for a 24-year-old." — DM, June

---

## Who Is and Isn't a Fit

**They need:**
1. Willingness to train 3x per week, 40 minutes
2. Access to a basic gym or a pair of dumbbells at home

**They don't need:**
- Any current fitness level — the program starts from zero

**Not a fit:**
- Guys chasing a 6-pack in 6 weeks. This is a 12-month rebuild sold in 12-week blocks.

---

## Core Pain Points

### 1. Restart Shame
They've started and quit so many times that starting again feels embarrassing. This showed up in 6 of 8 calls.

> "Honestly the worst part is telling my wife I'm 'getting back into it' again. She just nods now." — Dave, call 3

### 2. Plans Built for Someone Else's Life
Every program they've tried assumed time and recovery they don't have.

> "Every plan I've bought was written for a 24-year-old." — DM, June

### 3. Invisible Decline
No crisis, just a slow slide. Nothing forces the change, so it never happens.

> "There was no wake-up call. I just slowly became a guy who gets winded on the stairs." — testimonial, Chris

## Why They'd Buy From You Specifically

### 1. You're one of them
Sam is 41 with two kids and posts his own 40-minute sessions from a garage gym.

> "You're the first fitness guy I follow who looks like he has a mortgage." — comment, May

### 2. The 3x40 promise
The program's constraint IS the marketing. Three sessions, forty minutes, nothing else.

## Common Objections

| Objection | What's Really Going On |
|---|---|
| "I'll start after summer / the busy season" | Fear of failing again, dressed up as scheduling |
| "Will this work if I can only train at home?" | Testing whether the plan bends to their life or demands a new one |
| "I've tried programs before" | They're asking you to explain why this time is different |

---

## Content Angles

- Lead with "back", never "transformation" — their word for the goal
- Restart Shame is the deepest nerve and nobody else in the niche talks about it
- Post real garage sessions with the kids interrupting — "looks like he has a mortgage" is the trust signal
- TO VALIDATE: health-scare angle — gather evidence before building content on it
